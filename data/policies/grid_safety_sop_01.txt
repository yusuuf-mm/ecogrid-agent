GRID SAFETY STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE 01
Effective: 2024-01-01
Authority: Regional Grid Operations Authority
Document ID: grid_safety_sop_01
Revision: 4.2

Section 1 — General Applicability

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) governs the routine operating
requirements for all battery energy storage systems (BESS) and distributed
energy resources interconnected with the regional distribution network under
normal grid conditions. The provisions herein apply whenever no advisory,
anomaly, or critical infrastructure event has been declared by the Authority.

Section 2 — Definitions

2.1  "State of Charge (SoC)" means the ratio of energy currently stored in a
     battery system to its nameplate usable capacity, expressed as a value
     between 0.0 and 1.0 inclusive.

2.2  "Minimum State of Charge Buffer" means the floor below which the SoC of
     any interconnected storage asset shall not fall during the operating
     window.

2.3  "Operating Window" means the continuous 24-hour planning horizon
     beginning at 00:00 local prevailing time.

Section 3 — Operating Requirements

3.1  Standard Operating Conditions. Under normal grid conditions, every
     interconnected BESS shall maintain a minimum state of charge buffer of
     10% of nameplate usable capacity at all hours of the operating window.

3.2  Charge Coordination. Charging from the grid shall be scheduled during
     hours of locational marginal price (LMP) below the rolling 24-hour mean
     unless doing so would violate Section 3.1.

3.3  Discharge Coordination. Discharge to the grid shall be permitted only
     during hours of LMP above the rolling 24-hour mean, subject to the
     Section 3.1 reserve constraint.

Section 4 — Compliance and Reporting

4.1  Operators shall retain hourly SoC telemetry for a period of not less
     than ninety (90) days and shall produce such records upon request by
     the Authority.

4.2  Failure to maintain the minimum state of charge buffer defined in
     Section 3.1 during standard operating conditions constitutes a
     reportable deviation and shall be disclosed in the next regularly
     scheduled compliance filing.

Section 5 — Effective Period

This procedure remains in force until rescinded, superseded, or modified
by subsequent SOP issuance.
