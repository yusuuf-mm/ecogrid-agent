GRID SAFETY STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE 04
Effective: 2024-01-01
Authority: Regional Grid Operations Authority
Document ID: grid_safety_sop_04
Revision: 3.0

Section 1 — Declaration of Anomaly

This Standard Operating Procedure is invoked upon formal declaration of a
Grid Anomaly by the Authority. A Grid Anomaly is defined as a deviation
from nominal operating parameters of the distribution network sufficient
to materially elevate the risk of cascading failure, including but not
limited to: forced transmission outages, generation shortfall events,
frequency excursions exceeding defined limits, or cyber-physical
incidents affecting grid control systems.

Section 2 — Anomaly Triggers

2.1  Authority-declared Grid Anomalies shall be communicated through all
     standard operator channels and shall specify the start time,
     anticipated duration, and geographic scope of the anomaly window.

2.2  Upon activation of this procedure, the reserve requirements of
     SOP 01, SOP 02, and SOP 03 (where applicable) are superseded for
     the duration of the anomaly window as set forth in Section 3.

Section 3 — Emergency Reserve Requirements

3.1  During any declared Grid Anomaly, every interconnected battery
     energy storage system shall maintain a minimum state of charge
     buffer of 35% of nameplate usable capacity at all hours of the
     operating window for the duration of the anomaly.

3.2  The Section 3.1 reserve is sized to enable coordinated black-start
     support and to maintain frequency response capability for a minimum
     of six (6) consecutive hours.

3.3  Discharge to the grid during an active anomaly shall be permitted
     only when explicitly directed by the Authority or when required to
     prevent imminent breach of the Section 3.1 floor due to unplanned
     load events.

Section 4 — Operator Obligations

4.1  Operators shall, upon notification of anomaly activation, freeze all
     non-essential discharge schedules and shall re-position assets to
     or above the Section 3.1 floor within sixty (60) minutes.

4.2  Operators shall maintain continuous staffed monitoring of covered
     assets throughout the anomaly window and shall report any breach
     of the Section 3.1 floor to the Authority within fifteen (15)
     minutes of occurrence.

Section 5 — Deactivation

This procedure deactivates upon formal rescission of the underlying
anomaly declaration by the Authority. Upon deactivation, the reserve
requirement reverts to the appropriate standard procedure based on the
prevailing grid state.
