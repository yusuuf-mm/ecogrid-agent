GRID SAFETY STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE 02
Effective: 2024-06-01
Authority: Regional Grid Operations Authority
Document ID: grid_safety_sop_02
Revision: 1.1

Section 1 — Applicability and Trigger Conditions

This Standard Operating Procedure is issued in response to seasonal thermal
load conditions and is activated upon issuance of a Summer Heat Advisory by
the Authority. Activation shall be communicated through standard operator
notification channels and shall remain in force for the duration of the
declared advisory period.

Section 2 — Seasonal Risk Context

2.1  Summer Heat Advisory periods are characterized by sustained ambient
     temperatures in excess of 35 degrees Celsius for three (3) or more
     consecutive hours, accompanied by elevated system-wide load factors.

2.2  During such periods, the probability of unplanned transmission
     constraint events increases materially and the Authority requires
     elevated storage reserves to manage potential frequency deviations.

Section 3 — Elevated Reserve Requirements

3.1  During any active Summer Heat Advisory, every interconnected battery
     energy storage system shall maintain a minimum state of charge buffer
     of 20% of nameplate usable capacity at all hours of the operating
     window.

3.2  The reserve specified in Section 3.1 is additive to any standing
     requirement of SOP 01 and shall supersede the Section 3.1 reserve of
     SOP 01 for the duration of the advisory.

3.3  Operators shall, where physically capable, pre-position storage assets
     at or above the Section 3.1 floor prior to the start of the advisory
     window.

Section 4 — Coordination with Market Activities

4.1  Charge scheduling under an active advisory shall prioritize system
     stability over revenue optimization. Operators electing to discharge
     to the grid during an advisory shall retain documentary evidence that
     the discharge did not reduce SoC below the Section 3.1 floor.

4.2  Repeated breaches of the Section 3.1 reserve during advisory periods
     shall be referred to the Compliance Office for review.

Section 5 — Deactivation

This procedure deactivates automatically upon formal rescission of the
underlying Summer Heat Advisory by the Authority. Upon deactivation, the
reserve requirement reverts to that defined in SOP 01.
