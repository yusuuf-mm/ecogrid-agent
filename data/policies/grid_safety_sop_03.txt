GRID SAFETY STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE 03
Effective: 2024-03-15
Authority: Regional Grid Operations Authority
Document ID: grid_safety_sop_03
Revision: 2.0

Section 1 — Scope and Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes elevated State of Charge
reserve requirements applicable to battery energy storage systems directly
supporting facilities designated as Critical Infrastructure under the
Regional Resilience Charter. The purpose of this procedure is to ensure
uninterruptible energy availability for life-safety and public-welfare
functions during grid stress events.

Section 2 — Covered Facilities

2.1  For the purposes of this procedure, Critical Infrastructure shall
     include, without limitation:
       (a) Acute care hospitals and trauma centers;
       (b) Emergency services dispatch and 911 operations centers;
       (c) Municipal water treatment and pumping stations;
       (d) Public transit traction power substations;
       (e) Designated emergency shelters operated by municipal authorities.

2.2  Operators of storage assets that are electrically co-located with or
     contracted to support a facility enumerated in Section 2.1 shall
     comply with this procedure at all times regardless of the prevailing
     operating mode of the wider grid.

Section 3 — Hospital Reserve Requirements

3.1  Storage assets subject to this procedure shall maintain a minimum
     state of charge buffer of 30% of nameplate usable capacity at all
     hours of the operating window.

3.2  The reserve specified in Section 3.1 represents the floor sufficient
     to maintain the connected critical load for a minimum of four (4)
     consecutive hours at the facility's average demand profile.

3.3  Where the connected facility is an acute care hospital, the reserve
     shall not be reduced below the Section 3.1 floor under any
     circumstance, including but not limited to the issuance of higher
     revenue market signals, force majeure declarations, or operator
     discretion.

Section 4 — Verification and Audit

4.1  Operators shall submit quarterly attestations confirming that each
     covered asset has maintained the Section 3.1 reserve continuously
     throughout the reporting period.

4.2  The Authority may, at its discretion, audit any covered asset by
     requesting sub-hourly telemetry for the immediately preceding thirty
     (30) day period.

Section 5 — Supersession

This procedure supersedes the reserve requirements of SOP 01 and SOP 02
for all assets within its scope. For assets outside the scope of Section
2.1, the applicable procedure shall be determined by the prevailing grid
state.
