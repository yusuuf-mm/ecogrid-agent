GRID SAFETY STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE 05
Effective: 2024-09-01
Authority: Regional Grid Operations Authority
Document ID: grid_safety_sop_05
Revision: 1.0

Section 1 — Maintenance Window Context

This Standard Operating Procedure governs the operating requirements for
interconnected battery energy storage systems during a scheduled
Maintenance Window declared by the Authority. A Maintenance Window is a
planned period during which transmission, distribution, or generation
assets are intentionally removed from service for inspection, testing,
or capital improvement activities.

Section 2 — Window Characteristics

2.1  Maintenance Windows are pre-scheduled and shall be communicated to
     affected operators not less than seventy-two (72) hours in advance
     except in the case of emergency repair under Section 2.2.

2.2  Emergency repair windows may be declared with shorter notice when
     unplanned equipment failure creates immediate safety or reliability
     risk; this procedure applies equally to planned and emergency
     windows.

Section 3 — Reduced Reserve Requirements

3.1  During an active Maintenance Window, every interconnected battery
     energy storage system shall maintain a minimum state of charge
     buffer of 5% of nameplate usable capacity at all hours of the
     operating window.

3.2  The Section 3.1 reserve reflects the reduced contingency risk
     associated with controlled, scheduled outages and is intended to
     free storage capacity for revenue optimization and for arbitrage
     against predictable intra-day price differentials created by the
     maintenance activity itself.

3.3  Operators shall not interpret the Section 3.1 reduction as
     authorization to violate any binding interconnection agreement,
     warranty condition, or asset-level safety limit. Where multiple
     requirements conflict, the most conservative shall govern.

Section 4 — Reapplication of Standard Procedures

4.1  Upon conclusion of the Maintenance Window, the reserve requirement
     reverts immediately to the appropriate standard procedure based
     on the prevailing grid state.

4.2  Operators shall report any event occurring within a Maintenance
     Window that would, outside the window, have constituted a reportable
     deviation, within twenty-four (24) hours of window conclusion.

Section 5 — Coordination with Market Operator

The Authority shall coordinate Maintenance Window scheduling with the
applicable market operator to ensure that reserve reductions are
reflected in real-time market dispatch instructions issued to covered
assets.
